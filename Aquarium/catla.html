Kingdom: Animalia Phylum: Chordata
Class: Actinopterygii Order: Cypriniformes
Family: Cyprinidae Subfamily: Labeoninae
Genus: Labeo Species: L. catla

Catla fish, scientifically known as *Catla catla*, is a prominent freshwater species that holds significant economic and ecological importance in South Asia, particularly in countries like India, Bangladesh, and Nepal. This fish is easily recognizable due to its distinctive features, including a broad, flat head and a deep, laterally compressed body, which can reach impressive sizes of up to 2 meters in length and weigh over 100 kilograms. Catla fish are primarily herbivorous, feeding on phytoplankton, aquatic plants, and detritus, which positions them as a vital component of the freshwater ecosystem, contributing to nutrient cycling and maintaining ecological balance. Their rapid growth rate and adaptability to various environmental conditions make them a popular choice for aquaculture, where they are farmed extensively in ponds and reservoirs. The flesh of catla fish is highly prized for its tender texture and mild flavor, making it a staple in many regional cuisines and a favorite among consumers. In addition to their culinary appeal, catla fish play a crucial role in local fisheries, supporting the livelihoods of countless communities that rely on fishing as a primary source of income. The increasing demand for catla fish has led to a surge in aquaculture practices, prompting efforts to implement sustainable farming techniques that ensure the long-term viability of this species while minimizing environmental impact. As a result, catla fish not only contribute to food security and economic development but also highlight the importance of responsible resource management in preserving aquatic biodiversity for future generations. Their cultural significance, coupled with their ecological role, underscores the need for continued research and conservation efforts to protect this valuable species and its habitat in the face of growing environmental challenges.
